{% trans "What is Industrial Production Index (IPI)?" %}

{% blocktrans %} Industrial Production Index (IPI) measures the real output of the three main industrial sectors in Malaysia: mining, manufacturing, and electricity. It serves as a key indicator of industrial activity and economic performance. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} For this analysis, we focus on how Malaysia's diesel subsidy policy affects the manufacturing sector, which contributes approximately 23% to the country's GDP and employs over 2.2 million workers. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Why These Sectors Are More Affected" %}

{% trans "The sectors most impacted by the diesel subsidy policy changes share key characteristics:" %}

  • {% trans "High energy intensity:" %} {% trans "Industries like Fabricated Metal Products and Chemicals and Chemical Products require large amounts of fuel for manufacturing processes." %}
  • {% trans "Dependence on diesel-powered machinery:" %} {% trans "Sectors such as Machinery Repair and Installation rely directly on diesel for operational equipment." %}
  • {% trans "Reliance on road transport:" %} {% trans "Industries like Wearing Apparel and Rubber and Plastics Products depend on logistics networks powered by diesel for raw materials and product delivery." %}
  • {% trans "Fuel-sensitive supply chains:" %} {% trans "Sectors such as Computer, Electronic, and Optical Products are part of global supply chains where fuel costs can significantly impact competitiveness." %}
  • {% trans "Limited alternatives to diesel:" %} {% trans "For sectors like Coke and Refined Petroleum Products, diesel is not just a cost but also a core input, leaving little flexibility in adapting to price shocks." %}

{% trans "Economic Implications" %}

{% blocktrans %} The different responses between export and domestic-oriented manufacturing have important implications: {% endblocktrans %}

  • {% trans "International Competitiveness:" %} {% trans "Export sectors face greater challenges as they cannot easily pass increased costs to international buyers in competitive markets" %}
  • {% trans "Consumer Prices:" %} {% trans "Domestic manufacturing sectors can partially pass costs to local consumers, contributing to inflationary pressure" %}
  • {% trans "Employment Effects:" %} {% trans "Export sectors typically employ more workers, so their volatility can have larger employment impacts" %}
  • {% trans "Policy Considerations:" %} {% trans "Targeted support mechanisms may be needed for export sectors to maintain international competitiveness" %}
