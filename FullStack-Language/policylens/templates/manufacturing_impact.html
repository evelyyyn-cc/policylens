What is Industrial Production Index (IPI)?

Industrial Production Index (IPI) measures the real output of the three main industrial sectors in Malaysia: mining, manufacturing, and electricity. It serves as a key indicator of industrial activity and economic performance.

For this analysis, we focus on how Malaysia's diesel subsidy policy affects the manufacturing sector, which contributes approximately 23% to the country's GDP and employs over 2.2 million workers.

Overall Manufacturing Performance Trend

This chart shows the manufacturing index trends before and after key diesel subsidy policy changes.

The chart below shows Malaysia's manufacturing performance from 2023 to early 2025, highlighting the periods before and after major diesel subsidy policy implementations. The absolute index values (2015=100) illustrate the sector's overall performance trajectory.

Key Observations:

  • Apr–Jul 2023: Manufacturing output plunged by 2.1 % y/y at its weakest point, driven primarily by softer global demand, semiconductor supply-chain disruptions, and firms' destocking—policy-driven fuel costs played no part.
  • Aug–Dec 2023: Output stabilized around flat (±0.1 % y/y) as companies implemented tighter working-capital management, deferred non-critical capital expenditures, and shifted toward just-in-time inventories to preserve cash flow.
  • Jan 2024: The government's announcement of a move to targeted diesel subsidies prompted manufacturers to hedge fuel exposure and renegotiate long-term transport contracts, signaling a shift in cost-management strategies even before prices changed.
  • 10 Jun 2024: The blanket diesel subsidy was removed in Peninsular Malaysia (price rose from RM 2.15 to RM 3.35/L), increasing average logistics and on-site energy costs by roughly 30 %. Manufacturers responded with accelerated cost-pass-through, renegotiated supplier rates, and increased automation to offset higher fuel expenses.
  • Jul 2024–Jan 2025: Despite higher diesel prices, output rebounded strongly (+3 %–5 % y/y), driven by renewed export orders, strategic inventory restocking, and a 4 % improvement in energy intensity per unit of output—underscoring the sector's resilience and adaptability.
Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia, Industrial Production Index (IPI) Data, Feb 2025

Why These Sectors Are More Affected

The sectors most impacted by diesel subsidy policy changes share common characteristics:

  • Energy Intensity: Basic metals and chemical manufacturing require substantial energy inputs
  • Transport Dependencies: Food products rely heavily on transportation networks
  • Petroleum-Based Inputs: Chemicals and rubber/plastics use petroleum derivatives as raw materials
  • Limited Short-term Alternatives: These sectors have fewer immediate options to adapt their energy sources
Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia, IPI by Division (2 digit) Data, Feb 2025

Diesel Price Impact Analysis

This comparison shows how export and domestic-oriented sectors respond differently to diesel subsidy changes.

Malaysia's manufacturing sector can be broadly divided into export-oriented industries (which produce mainly for international markets) and domestic-oriented industries (which serve the local market). Each responds differently to diesel subsidy policy changes.

Economic Implications

The different responses between export and domestic-oriented manufacturing have important implications:

  • International Competitiveness: Export sectors face greater challenges as they cannot easily pass increased costs to international buyers in competitive markets
  • Consumer Prices: Domestic manufacturing sectors can partially pass costs to local consumers, contributing to inflationary pressure
  • Employment Effects: Export sectors typically employ more workers, so their volatility can have larger employment impacts
  • Policy Considerations: Targeted support mechanisms may be needed for export sectors to maintain international competitiveness
Source: Department of Statistics Malaysia, IPI for Export-Oriented Divisions and IPI for Domestic-Oriented Divisions Data, Feb 2025
